🎓 Micro Lesson: Understanding Your Outcome Scores

What Are Outcomes?

In AUTOA 123, every assignment and lab is tied to specific learning outcomes — the actual skills and tasks you need to demonstrate to become a competent automotive technician.

These outcomes come directly from the ASE Education Foundation task list — the same industry standard used to certify automotive programs across the country. When you demonstrate mastery of an outcome, it means you can actually do that task in a real shop.

Example outcome: VI.B.2 — Confirm proper battery capacity for vehicle application; perform battery capacity and load test; determine needed action.

Your grade in this course reflects two things — your points on assignments AND your demonstrated skill level on each outcome. You can track both in Canvas.

The Proficiency Scale — What Your Score Means

Each outcome is scored on a 0–4 scale. Here is exactly what each level means in this course:

Mastery
Demonstrates the skill independently and correctly
4 pts
 Near Mastery
Demonstrates with minor errors, meets the bar
3 pts
 Developing
Understands but needs prompting or assistance
2 pts
 Attempted
Engaged but fundamental errors present, needs intervention
1 pt
 Not Demonstrated
No meaningful attempt, skipped, or unsafe approach
0 pts
⚠ Important: The threshold for this course is 3 — Near Mastery. That is the minimum expectation. A 2 means you are on your way but not there yet. A 0 for an unsafe approach is taken seriously — safety is non-negotiable in the shop.

Where to Find Your Outcome Scores in Canvas

Canvas shows you two gradebooks side by side — your regular assignment grades AND your outcome proficiency scores. Here is how to find them:

  • Go to Grades in the left navigation of this course
  • At the top of the gradebook you will see two tabs — Grades and Learning Mastery
  • Click Learning Mastery to see your proficiency score on every outcome
  • Each outcome shows your current level — color coded from red (Not Demonstrated) to green (Mastery)
Why this matters: Your outcome scores update as you complete assignments throughout the quarter. If you are below Near Mastery (3) on an outcome, that is a signal to get more practice before the end of the quarter — not something to ignore.
📌 Heads up: Your outcome score may be calculated as a decaying average — meaning your most recent performance counts more than earlier attempts. Improving over time is reflected in your score.
