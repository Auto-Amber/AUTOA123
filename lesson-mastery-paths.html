🎓 Micro Lesson: Canvas Mastery Paths

What Are Mastery Paths?

Mastery Paths is a Canvas feature that automatically routes students to different content based on how they perform on an assignment or quiz. Instead of every student doing the same next thing, Canvas sends them to what they actually need.

Simple version: You score below 70% on the quiz → Canvas automatically assigns you the review materials. You score above 70% → Canvas sends you to the extension activity. It happens automatically.

This is especially useful in our class because students come in with very different preparation levels. Mastery Paths lets the course adapt to each student without extra work from you or the instructor.

📌 In AUTOA 123: Mastery Paths are being set up so that students who need more practice get targeted review, and students who are ready move forward. You may see different assignments than your classmates — that is intentional and normal.

How Does It Work?

Mastery Paths work in three steps:

  • Step 1 — Trigger assignment: You complete a quiz or assignment (the trigger). Canvas scores it.
  • Step 2 — Canvas evaluates your score: Your score falls into a range the instructor set up (e.g., 0–69%, 70–84%, 85–100%).
  • Step 3 — New content unlocks: Canvas automatically makes the right follow-up content available to you based on your score range.

What This Means for You as a Student

Here is what to expect when Mastery Paths are active in your course:

  • Some assignments may be locked until you complete the trigger assignment — this is normal
  • Your Modules page may look different from a classmate's — you are seeing your personalized path
  • If you get review materials, it is not a punishment — it means the system identified a gap and is helping you fill it before moving on
  • If you unlock an extension, it means you demonstrated mastery — take advantage of it
  • You can always ask your instructor which path you are on and why
Bottom line: Mastery Paths exist to make sure no student gets left behind AND no student is held back. Your path is based on your performance — not a judgment about your ability.
