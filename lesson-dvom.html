⚡ Micro Lesson: How a DVOM Tests Circuits

What Does a DVOM Actually Do?

A DVOM (Digital Volt-Ohm Meter) is your most important diagnostic tool. It measures three fundamental electrical values — and how you connect it changes what it measures and how the circuit behaves during the test.

Voltage (V)

Measures electrical pressure. Connected in parallel. Circuit stays live.

Resistance (Ω)

Measures opposition to flow. Circuit must be off and unpowered.

Current (A/mA)

Measures flow rate. Connected in series. Circuit must be broken to insert meter.

⚠ Key rule: Voltage and resistance are measured very differently. Mixing them up can blow the fuse in your meter or damage the circuit.

Voltage Testing — Parallel Connection

When measuring voltage, the DVOM is connected in parallel — both leads touch two points in the circuit without breaking it. The meter has very high internal resistance so it barely draws any current.

  • Voltage present test: Check if power is reaching a component
  • Voltage drop test: Measure loss across a connection or wire
  • Reference voltage: Check sensor supply voltage (typically 5V)
Remember: Red lead = positive test point. Black lead = ground or negative reference. The meter reads the difference between the two points.
2 / 4

Resistance Testing — Circuit Must Be Off

When measuring resistance, the DVOM sends its own small current through the component and measures the opposition. This means the circuit must be unpowered — otherwise the meter reading is wrong and you risk damaging the meter.

🚫 Never measure resistance on a live circuit. The meter's internal voltage combined with external voltage will give a false reading and can damage the meter.
  • Continuity test: Does the wire/connection have a complete path? (near 0Ω = good)
  • Component resistance: Is the sensor, coil, or motor within spec?
  • Isolation test: Is there unwanted continuity between circuits? (OL = good)
OL (Over Limit) on resistance = open circuit — no continuity. This is normal when testing for isolation, but a problem when you expect a complete path.
