⚡ Micro Lesson: Diodes

What is a Diode?

A diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow in one direction only. Think of it like a one-way check valve in a cooling system — fluid (current) can go through one way, but is blocked coming back.

Key Term: Diode — a semiconductor device that permits current flow in one direction only.

The pointed end of the triangle symbol shows the direction current flows. The bar (cathode) blocks reverse current.

Where Are Diodes Used in Vehicles?

Diodes show up in several places in automotive electrical systems. The most important one you'll test regularly is inside the alternator.

  • Alternator rectifier diodes — convert AC to DC so the battery can be charged
  • Isolation diodes — prevent current from flowing backward between circuits
  • Clamping/flyback diodes — protect circuits from voltage spikes when relays switch off
  • LED lighting — LEDs are diodes that emit light when forward biased
⚠ Shop tip: A bad diode in the alternator is one of the most common causes of a battery drain complaint. The alternator passes current backward through the dead diode when the engine is off.

Testing a Diode with a DVOM

Your DVOM has a diode test setting — it looks like a triangle with a bar (same as the schematic symbol). Here's how to use it:

  • Forward bias test: Red lead to anode, black to cathode → should read 0.4–0.7V (silicon diode)
  • Reverse bias test: Swap leads → should read OL (open loop — blocked)
Good diode: Low reading one way, OL the other way.
Shorted diode: Low reading BOTH ways — current flows both directions (bad).
Open diode: OL BOTH ways — no current flows at all (bad).
